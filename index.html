I am a PhD student at Aarhus University, where I research contemporary digital media: digital infrastructures, smart cities, Big Data
(and a lot in-between).


My PhD project is entitled “Embedded Digital Infrastructures: a critical study of Smart Cities’ Application Programming Interfaces” (supervised by Prof. Annette Markham). I aim to study how APIs (Application Programming Interfaces) function invisibly under the surface of "smart cities" to connect and control data and information flow between technologies such as of sensors, web interfaces, and automated city systems (traffic lights, CCTV, citizen registries). The analytical goal is to surface the invisible work of APIs, exploring how these infrastructures also have politics; how they change, constrain or enable our lives in specific ways. I have been interested in digital/data infrastructures, internet research and software studies, especially through research methods that creatively blend science and technology theories with artistic practice (audiovisual, software and media arts).

I am part of the Digital Living Research Commons and participate in projects such as the Museum of Random Memory, one of a series of experiments by the Future Making Consortium to "identify and foster ongoing and new creative methods of regaining control of the ‘big data’ we regularly produce in our everyday lives"; and the All The Feels Research Group, which employs different methodological approaches (phenomenology, discourse analysis, etc) to explore how young people make sense of their social media use. From 2018 to 2019, I am a Research Fellow at the Center for Arts, Design, and Social Research (CAD+SR, Boston, USA). You can also check my other projects and follow my blog .

Selected Projects

Museum of Random Memory

MoRM is an ongoing series of artistic interventions and installations that researches the 'big data' we produce in our daily lives. Alongside the MoRM uncurators, I was responsible for designing and programming the MoRM site. Presented at: Counterplay (Aarhus, 2016/2017), Museu del Disseny (Barcelona, 2017) Festival of Research (Aarhus 2017), and CAD+SR (Orvieto, 2017).

Another 33rd São Paulo Biennial

Collaboration with Bruno Moreschi's project for the 33rd Sao Paulo Art Biennial. This project creates alternative modes of comprehending the exhibition that go against the official discourse, such as through Artificial Intelligence analysis of the Biennial’s images; audio guide tracks with the commentary of guards, exhibit technicians, and cleaning workers; unconventional recordings of the setup; records of the reactions of the public etc.


Virtual Ideological Noises in the Van Abbemuseum Collection

Previous experiences in institutional critique and the idea of emancipated experiences in museums led artist Bruno Moreschi and the researcher partner Gabriel Pereira to the virtual layers of museums. The research proposal is to analyse how various technological 'machine learning' tools behave with images from the Van Abbe museum collection.

HISTORY OF _RT

HISTORY OF _RT presents quantitative and qualitative data about 2.443 artists of 11 books used in Art History courses in Universities in Brazil. The project, funded by Itaú Rumos, includes a website visualization, printed material and exhibits. Concept: Amália dos Santos, Bruno Moreschi and Gabriel Pereira. historyof-rt.org
