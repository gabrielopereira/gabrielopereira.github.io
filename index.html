I'm a student researcher at Aarhus University, where I research contemporary digital media: digital infrastructures, smart cities, Big Data
(and a lot in-between).


I currently study in the Information Studies - Digital Living Masters program at Aarhus University. I have been interested in digital infrastructures, internet research and software studies, especially through research methods that creatively blend science and technology theories with artistic practice (audiovisual, software and media arts).

I'm Prof. Annette Markham's Research Assistant and participate in projects such as the Museum of Random Memory, one of a series of experiments by the Future Making Consortium and the Digital Living Research Commons to "identify and foster ongoing and new creative methods of regaining control of the ‘big data’ we regularly produce in our everyday lives", and the All The Feels Research Group. You can also check my other projects and follow my blog .

Multimedia

Museum of Random Memory

Alongside the UI/UX Team, I was responsible for designing and programming the MoRM site. MoRM is an artistic research intervention that generates questions about the 'big data' we produce in our daily lives.

Presented at: Counterplay (Aarhus, 2016/2017) and Museu del Disseny (Barcelona, 2017).

HISTORY OF _RT

HISTORY OF _RT presents quantitative and qualitative data about 2.443 artists of 11 books used in Art History courses in Universities in Brazil. The project, funded by Itaú Rumos, includes a forthcoming website visualization, printed material and exhibits.

Concept: Amália dos Santos, Bruno Moreschi and Gabriel Pereira.
